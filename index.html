Fiction books

"Fiction" refers to literature created from the imagination. Mysteries, science fiction, romance, fantasy, chick lit, crime thrillers are all fiction genres.


Amor Towles, "The Lincoln Highway: A Novel"


George Orwell, "1984"

Non-fiction books

Nonfiction presents only truth and accuracy regarding information, events, people.


Mo Gawdat, "Scary Smart"

Michelle Alexander, The New Jim Crow

Drama books

The drama genre is strongly based in a character, or characters, that are in conflict at a crucial moment in their lives.


William Shakespeare, "Hamlet"

Samuel Beckett, "Waiting for Godot"

Poetry books

A poetic genre is generally a tradition or classification of poetry based on the subject matter, style, or other broader literary characteristics.


Atticus Poetry, "Love Her Wild"

Kaur Rupi, "Milk and Honey"

Folktale books

Folktales are stories in the oral tradition, or tales that people tell each other out loud, rather than stories in written form. Here you can find stories with illustrations.


Tomie dePaolo "The Legend of the Bluebonnet"

Jonathan Allen, "The little red hen"
